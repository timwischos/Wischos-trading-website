# Ideal Customer Profile — Wischos Gift

## 1. Overview

Wischos Gift sells custom metal gift sets to the operational layer of SMBs — the people who run corporate gifting programs without a dedicated procurement team. We solve the problem of finding premium, branded gifts at small quantities (100 sets) that look considered rather than promotional. Buyers choose Wischos Gift over Alibaba because we handle packaging design, MOQ fits their scale, and they deal with one professional counterpart throughout.

---

## 2. Buyer Personas

### Office Manager

**Typical job titles:** Office Manager, Operations Manager, Facilities Coordinator, Office Administrator

**Company size sweet spot:** 50–300 employees

**Primary pain point:** Has no gifting supplier relationship and Googles ad-hoc before every gift program — always worried the items will look cheap and embarrass the company in front of clients.

**How they discover vendors:**
- Google search ("custom corporate gift sets Australia", "branded metal gifts bulk")
- LinkedIn DMs from vendors (receptive if message is relevant)
- Peer recommendations from other office managers in same industry network

**Decision speed and sign-off:** Fast — days, not weeks. Typically owns the budget line ($2,000–$8,000) without executive sign-off below that threshold.

**Priority geographies:** Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane (AU); Amsterdam, Rotterdam (NL); Hamburg, Berlin (DE); Singapore

**Does NOT fit:**
- Budget is under $1,500 total (price per unit math doesn't work at MOQ 100)
- Company has a procurement policy requiring three supplier quotes and a formal PO process

---

### Executive Assistant / EA

**Typical job titles:** Executive Assistant, EA to CEO, Personal Assistant, Chief of Staff

**Company size sweet spot:** 100–500 employees

**Primary pain point:** Buys gifts on behalf of executives where failure is visible and success is invisible — needs physical proof (samples or photos) to show the executive before committing, and works with short, externally imposed deadlines.

**How they discover vendors:**
- LinkedIn personal message from a vendor (most effective channel for this persona)
- Referral from another EA or PA in their network
- Google when under time pressure and no network referral available

**Decision speed and sign-off:** Medium — 1 to 2 weeks. Requires executive sign-off on concept (usually informal approval of sample photo or mockup, not a formal purchase order review).

**Priority geographies:** Sydney, Melbourne (AU); London (UK); Amsterdam (NL); all target markets respond but AU and UK are strongest.

**Does NOT fit:**
- Executive is the direct contact (EA is the gatekeeper; reaching out to the executive directly skips the actual decision influencer)
- Company events budget is controlled by a dedicated events team (different buyer role entirely)

---

### HR Manager / People Ops

**Typical job titles:** HR Manager, Head of People, People Operations Manager, Culture and Engagement Lead

**Company size sweet spot:** 100–500 employees

**Primary pain point:** Running onboarding gift programs that need to scale to 100+ new hires per year, with branded items that feel premium — not the type of cheap pen set that signals the company does not care.

**How they discover vendors:**
- LinkedIn thought leadership posts (engage with content before buying)
- HR community forums and Slack groups (peer-to-peer referrals)
- Vendor outreach on LinkedIn when the profile shows expertise, not just product

**Decision speed and sign-off:** Slow — budget cycles, approval chains, annual planning timelines. Decision can take 4 to 8 weeks but produces higher-value orders (100–300 sets per year on a recurring basis).

**Priority geographies:** Singapore, South Korea (Seoul), Melbourne and Sydney (AU) — startup and scale-up density highest in these cities.

**Does NOT fit:**
- HR team is 1 person managing compliance only (no engagement/culture mandate, no gift budget)
- Company is in a cost-cutting phase (hiring freeze visible on LinkedIn job posts)

---

### Marketing Coordinator (Secondary)

**Typical job titles:** Marketing Coordinator, Brand Manager, Events Coordinator, Partnerships Manager

**Company size sweet spot:** 50–200 employees

**Primary pain point:** Needs event giveaways and conference booth items that look professional and reflect brand identity — not generic promotional merchandise that ends up in the bin.

**How they discover vendors:**
- Event industry referrals and supplier directories
- LinkedIn (searching for branded merchandise vendors when planning an event)
- Google when deadline is imminent

**Decision speed and sign-off:** Fast when event-driven — a conference in 6 weeks creates urgency. Slow otherwise (lower priority without a deadline).

**Priority geographies:** EU (Netherlands, Germany, UK), Australia; any market with active B2B event calendar.

**Does NOT fit:**
- Budget is for promotional FMCG-style giveaways (pens with logo printed for $1 each — wrong product tier)
- No upcoming events or campaign tied to the purchase (without a deadline, deprioritized indefinitely)

## 3. Firmographic Parameters

| Parameter | Target | Exclude |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Company size | 50–500 employees | <50 (informal/personal gifting, no budget); >500 (procurement committee, tender process) |
| Industries | Professional Services, Tech/SaaS, Finance, Legal, Real Estate, Healthcare (private) | Manufacturing, Retail, Government/Public Sector |
| Geographies (priority order) | Australia (Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane), EU (Netherlands, Germany, UK), Singapore, South Korea, Japan | USA (tariff complexity 2026), China (competitor market), India (price competition) |
| Annual revenue | $5M–$100M USD equivalent | <$2M (gifting is personal-scale, not corporate); >$500M (enterprise procurement) |
| Budget signal | Has organized any corporate event, client appreciation program, or onboarding kit program in past 12 months | No visible gifting or event activity |
| Decision structure | 1–2 person decision (e.g., Office Manager + CFO informal sign-off) | Full procurement committee with formal tender/RFQ process |

---

## 4. Qualification Checklist

Run this before reaching out to any prospect. All five must be true.

- [ ] Company is in a target geography (AU, EU/NL/DE/UK, SG, KR, JP)?
- [ ] Company size is 50–500 employees (verify on LinkedIn company page)?
- [ ] Industry is on the target list — not manufacturing, retail, or government?
- [ ] Contact role matches one of the four personas (Office Manager, EA, HR Manager, Marketing Coordinator)?
- [ ] Any visible gifting or event activity in past 12 months (LinkedIn posts mentioning gifts/swag/events, or job description mentions "coordinate gifts" or "manage office supplies")?

---

## 5. When NOT to Pursue

Stop and move on when any of these are true:

- Company has more than 500 employees (procurement department exists; selling cycle is 6+ months with no guarantee of outcome)
- Company is based in the USA (2026 tariff structure makes pricing uncompetitive without dedicated repositioning; not the current focus)
- Government or public sector entity (procurement rules, lowest-price bias, long approval chains)
- Retail or FMCG company (gifting budget goes to in-store promotions and GWP programs, not B2B gift sets)
- Only visible contact is C-suite (CEO, CFO, COO) with no operational layer — outreach will not reach the actual buyer
- Company founded in the past 6 months (no gifting budget established, likely no events yet)
- LinkedIn company page shows a hiring freeze or layoff news in the past 90 days (budget is not available)
