    The document is a copy-paste reference. Every search string must be in a fenced code block so the operator can copy it directly. Write in plain Markdown.

    **Section 1: How to Use This Playbook**
    3–4 sentences: These strings are for manual research in Google. Paste each string into google.com search box. Results surface public LinkedIn profiles and company pages. After finding candidates via Google, check their LinkedIn activity manually — prioritise profiles active in the past 30 days. This is prospecting research, not automated scraping.

    **Section 2: Operator Reference**
    A table of the 6 operators used throughout the playbook:
    | Operator | Function | Example |
    - site:linkedin.com/in/ — search only LinkedIn personal profiles
    - site:linkedin.com/company/ — search only LinkedIn company pages
    - intitle:"..." — page title contains exact phrase
    - "..." — exact phrase match anywhere on page
    - OR — boolean OR between two terms (must be uppercase)
    - - — exclude term from results

    **Section 3: Search Strings by Persona and Geography**

    Write at least 12 search strings total. Group by persona. Each string has a comment line above it (starting with #) naming the target persona and geography. All strings must be in a code block.

    Required coverage — write at least one string per row:

    OFFICE MANAGER persona:
    - AU: Sydney/Melbourne/Brisbane + professional services OR consulting OR tech (company size signal optional)
    - EU: Amsterdam/Rotterdam/Berlin/Hamburg + professional services OR consulting
    - SG: Singapore + technology OR fintech OR professional services

    EXECUTIVE ASSISTANT / EA persona:
    - AU: Sydney/Melbourne + financial services OR law OR real estate
    - EU: London/Amsterdam + consulting OR finance
    - JP: Tokyo + international OR global

    HR MANAGER / PEOPLE OPS persona:
    - SG: Singapore + technology OR fintech
    - KR: Seoul + technology OR startup
    - AU: Melbourne/Sydney + startup OR scale-up OR "people operations"

    COMPANY-LEVEL (finding target companies, not individuals):
    - AU: professional services companies with 51-200 employees
    - SG: technology companies with 51-200 or 201-500 employees
    - Bonus: companies with event/gifting signals

    For each string: use the patterns from 06-RESEARCH.md as starting points but write final strings that are specific, syntactically correct, and directly usable. The AU Office Manager string from research is:
    `site:linkedin.com/in/ "office manager" "sydney" OR "melbourne" OR "brisbane" "professional services" OR "consulting" OR "tech"`
    Use this as the model for structure and specificity.

    **Section 4: Post-Google LinkedIn Filtering**
    After running a search string, use these filters on the profiles found:
    - Check the profile's "Activity" tab — any posts in the past 30 days?
    - Look for mentions of "corporate gifts", "swag", "onboarding kit", "company gift" in their posts or About section
    - Check company size on company page — confirm 50–500 employees
    - Check company industry — confirm not government/retail/manufacturing
    If all checks pass: add to outreach shortlist.

    **Section 5: Outreach Starting Point**
    One short paragraph: After building the shortlist, reach out via LinkedIn DM. Keep the first message under 75 words. Lead with a specific observation about their company or role (not a generic opener). Mention wischosgift.com once with a specific product or use case relevant to their industry. Do not ask for a meeting — ask a question.

    Tone: Practical, no marketing language. Written for someone who will actually run these searches.
